I've been mentoring designers since 2009. Four of them are now design leads at Meta. Several at Booking, Uber, IBM, Accenture. Eleven countries' worth.

The math is easy: 1,000 mentees over 17 years is roughly one new conversation a week, every week, for two decades. The pattern is harder. Not everything that worked for the first hundred works for the next hundred.

## Four traps that don't scale

- The 1:1 trap. Hour-long calls scale linearly. Your hours don't. After 50 mentees, the 1:1 model breaks. Build cohort and group spaces.
- The dialect trap. What works for a US senior designer reads as career-ending advice for a junior in India. Translate, don't replicate.
- The lifecycle trap. The advice that gets a designer their first job is the wrong advice for promotion to senior. Stage your guidance.
- The ego trap. Mentees grow up. They will eventually disagree with you and be right. Invite that, plan for it, celebrate it.

## What actually scales

One belief, repeated. For me it's: design is a leadership problem. I say it the same way at the IDEO Creative Leadership program, in 1:1s, in conference talks, in cohort group calls. The repetition is the lesson.

> 1,000 mentees taught me the same thing 1,000 times. That was the point.
